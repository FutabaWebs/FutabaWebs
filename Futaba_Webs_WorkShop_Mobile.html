Yup! You’ve guessed it, I’m creating my own PET! The "Megaman Battle Network" series is what got me into programming. This is a tribute to the game that started it all. It also got me into hacking too! As you can tell already a lot of my projects are heavily themed based. I love bringing what I see to life. Wouldn’t you? Now if only I could bring a certain web slinger to life now that’d be great! Don’t you agree?

Why?:

I’m developing a penetration testing app that will incorporate machine learning as well as a “special” feature to create a net navi, or A.I. that will bring my project to life. Pen-Testing Drone:

Are you a lover of the "Watch Dogs"series? Check out this cool Penetration testing drone I'm working on inspired by Watch Dogs2, a tribute to the series. Think of it as your friendly neighborhood flying wifi-pineapple! The penetration testing drone from the watch dogs series has always fascinated me, so I’ve decided to bring the project to life. I mean, why not? It’s cool! A flying drone that can crack various wireless data encryptions and exfiltrate data. Currently in alpha phase.

Why?:

Although I have a love for quadcopters, I’ve always been disappointed by many of them, even DJI ones. Why? Because they don’t really do anything besides look pretty and have a nice camera; Oh and tricks. You pay so much for a drone that doesn’t do so much, and is practically copy and paste across the board. Drones should be out of this world cool. Let’s fix that!

The Project?:

The sole purpose of the drone is to fly around capturing packet data, as well as cracking Wireless encryptions, integrating the use of a raspberry pi zero to host my UDP BackDoor Server program in order to communicate with the drone and see what's on the pi as it's flying. When the drone boots up, the pi zero will auto login, where a set of scripts will run, starting up the UDP Server.The flight controller for the drone is built up from scratch, rather than using a pre-built one. The drone will take a series of commands from the transmitter, the controller, also built from scratch (keyboard input option available as well), and depending on the value read from the analog of the joystick module, will move the drone in the desired corresponding direction
